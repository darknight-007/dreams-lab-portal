Strategy Comparison

Random Sampling

  • ✓ Simple implementation
  • ✓ Unbiased coverage
  • ✗ Inefficient resource usage
  • ✗ May miss important regions

Grid-based Sampling

  • ✓ Systematic coverage
  • ✓ Predictable patterns
  • ✗ Rigid structure
  • ✗ May oversample low-interest areas

Information Gain-based

  • ✓ Adaptive to findings
  • ✓ Efficient resource usage
  • ✓ Prioritizes high-value areas
  • ✓ Optimal for limited resources

Uniform Sampling

  • ✓ Even distribution
  • ✓ Easy to implement
  • ✗ Ignores environment structure
  • ✗ Not adaptive to discoveries

Real-world Applications

Planetary Exploration

Mars rovers use information gain-based sampling to efficiently explore and collect samples with limited battery life and storage capacity.

Environmental Monitoring

Adaptive sampling for tracking pollution spread or monitoring wildlife populations with limited sensor networks.

Search and Rescue

Optimal resource allocation in time-critical missions where coverage must be balanced with probability of success.
