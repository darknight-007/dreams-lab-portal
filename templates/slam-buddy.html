2D SLAM Tutorial with Factor Graph Visualization

What is SLAM?

SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping) is the process of building a map of an environment while simultaneously estimating the robot's position within that map. The key mathematical model for SLAM is:

\[ x_t = f(x_{t-1}, u_t) + w_t, \quad z_t = h(x_t, l_i) + v_t \]
  • \(x_t\): Robot pose at time \(t\)
  • \(u_t\): Control input
  • \(z_t\): Measurement of a landmark \(l_i\)
  • \(w_t\) and \(v_t\): Process and observation noise

Factor Graph Representation

Factor graphs model the SLAM problem with nodes for robot poses and landmarks, connected by edges representing motion constraints and measurements.
